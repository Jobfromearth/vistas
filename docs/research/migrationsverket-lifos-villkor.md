# Lifos / rättsliga ställningstaganden villkor — compliance research (companion to `migrationsverket-villkor.md`)

Research date: 2026-07-19. This is a companion to `docs/research/migrationsverket-villkor.md` (2026-07-18), which verified migrationsverket.se's main-site text copyright notice but explicitly could **not** verify Lifos (lifos.migrationsverket.se) or rättsliga ställningstaganden (legal position PDFs) and recommended keeping that content under ADR-0004's conservative default pending a separate check — that is what this document does. Conducted against primary sources via direct HTTP fetches (curl, not a browser-rendering tool — Claude's WebFetch tool hit TLS errors against these Swedish government hosts in this session, so raw HTML/PDF bytes were pulled directly and read/parsed locally instead) of lifos.migrationsverket.se, www.migrationsverket.se, and lagen.nu's consolidated text of upphovsrättslagen (1960:729), plus two full legal-position PDFs downloaded and text-extracted in full. All Swedish quotes below are verbatim from the cited URL at fetch time; translations in parentheses are mine.

## Summary verdict

**This does not fully close the ADR-0004 gate the way the 2026-07-18 migrationsverket.se research did — no explicit Migrationsverket/Lifos statement of copyright or reuse terms for rättsliga ställningstaganden was found despite a real search effort — but it substantially de-risks the specific unknowns that research flagged, and it surfaces a stronger, different kind of argument than a permission notice: a statutory copyright-exemption argument, the same category the project already relies on for SFS law text.** Confirmed via primary sources: **(a)** Lifos is not a separate legal entity with its own (possibly hostile) terms — it is Migrationsverket's own database, operated by Migrationsverket's own internal units (Migrationsanalys for country information, rättsavdelningen for legal content), branded with Migrationsverket's logo linking back to www.migrationsverket.se, and sharing the parent site's accessibility statement — but it also has **no dedicated villkor/upphovsrätt page of its own**, and its navigation/footer never link to or reference the www.migrationsverket.se "Om webbplatsen" copyright notice, so that notice's applicability to Lifos-hosted PDFs is **unconfirmed and shouldn't be assumed by default**. **(b)** Rättsliga ställningstaganden are real, numbered, dated, versioned PDF documents published exclusively via lifos.migrationsverket.se's document-viewer pages — verified with two full-document downloads (RS/001/2024, RS/059/2021) and metadata checks on five documents spanning 2020–2026. **(c)** None of the checked documents or their landing pages carry any copyright notice, license statement, or confidentiality/"internal use"/classification marking — instead every one is tagged `publishingType: Publikt`, `accessControll: Publikt`, `limited: false` in Migrationsverket's own internal metadata, i.e. affirmatively **not** in the access-restricted ("åtkomstbegränsad") bucket that Lifos's own "Om Lifos" page says exists specifically for GDPR-, copyright-, or confidentiality-sensitive Lifos content. **(d)** The strongest finding is legal-structural, not permission-based: rättsliga ställningstaganden are Migrationsverket's own formally-decided ("Fastställelsebeslut"), dated, staff-binding institutional statements about how a statute should be interpreted — language that maps closely onto "beslut av myndigheter" and/or "yttranden av svenska myndigheter" in **upphovsrättslagen (1960:729) 9 §**, the exact provision the project's plan doc already invokes for SFS statute text being copyright-free. Even under the more conservative reading that a rättsligt ställningstagande is merely an ordinary "handling upprättad hos en myndighet" rather than a 9-§ "beslut"/"yttrande", **26 a § 2nd paragraph of the same Act** independently grants "var och en" (everyone) the right to freely reproduce authority-authored public documents that aren't already 9-§-exempt, subject to narrow named exclusions (maps, software, teaching/scientific-research works, art/music/poetry, commercially-sold works) that plainly do not describe a legal-position PDF. **No Swedish case law specifically classifying "ställningstaganden" (Migrationsverket's or, by analogy, Skatteverket's structurally-identical instrument) under §9 vs. §26a was found in this session**, so this remains a well-supported legal inference, not a confirmed holding.

**Recommendation:** don't flip the gate to full ingestion on the strength of an assumed permission — there still isn't one, and none should be assumed. Instead, reclassify the open question: stop asking "did Migrationsverket give permission to redistribute Lifos content" (no notice answers that) and bring the §9/§26a statutory-exemption finding above to the M5 pre-publication confirmatory Swedish-copyright-law review that `migrationsverket-villkor.md` already recommended getting before the irrevocable CC-BY/CC0 publication. If that review agrees rättsliga ställningstaganden fall under §9 or §26a (as the primary-source evidence here suggests), full-text ingestion becomes justified on the same "outside copyright, no license needed, attribute Migrationsverket as good practice" basis the project already uses for SFS text — not under the CC-BY licensing logic used for migrationsverket.se guidance prose. Until that review happens, ADR-0004's conservative default (abstract + structured facts + anchor link only) continues to apply to rättsliga ställningstaganden, even though the evidence gathered here is more favorable than the prior research's "found nothing" finding.

## 1. What is Lifos, structurally? Same entity/terms as migrationsverket.se, or separate?

**Lifos is Migrationsverket itself — not a separate legal entity — but it is also not simply "the same site" for terms-of-use purposes: it runs as its own distinct sub-site with its own "Om Lifos" page and no link back to the main site's copyright notice.**

- Homepage: https://lifos.migrationsverket.se/ (fetched directly, 2026-07-19)
- Self-description on the homepage: *"Lifos är Migrationsverkets databas för landinformation och rättslig styrning. Med hjälp av Lifos hittar du aktuell information och analys om länder och regioner från vilka människor söker sig till Sverige."* ("Lifos is Migrationsverket's database for country information and legal governance. Using Lifos you find current information and analysis about countries and regions from which people seek to come to Sweden.")
- The Lifos homepage logo links directly to `http://www.migrationsverket.se` with alt text *"Till Migrationsverkets webbplats"* ("To Migrationsverket's website") — confirms same organization, not a separate agency or contractor.
- "Om Lifos" page: https://lifos.migrationsverket.se/om-lifos.html (canonical `/aboutelifos/om-lifos.html`) states: *"Funktionen för land- och omvärldsanalys, Migrationsanalys, ansvarar för innehåll, drift och utveckling av databasen. Vi samlar in, analyserar, framställer och gör landinformation tillgänglig för aktörer inom migrationsområdet och allmänheten. Migrationsverkets rättsavdelning ansvarar för det rättsliga innehållet som framför allt publiceras i söktjänsten Rättsfallssamling."* ("The country-and-world-analysis function, Migrationsanalys, is responsible for content, operation, and development of the database... Migrationsverket's legal department is responsible for the legal content, published primarily in the Rättsfallssamling search service.") — both operating units are internal Migrationsverket departments, confirming Lifos is operated in-house, not by a third party.
- Lifos's footer links to the *main* site's shared accessibility statement (*"Tillgänglighetsredogörelse"* → `https://www.migrationsverket.se/Om-Migrationsverket/Om-webbplatsen/Tillganglighet/Tillganglighetsredogorelse.html`) — i.e. Lifos treats itself as covered by the same overarching legal-compliance page as www.migrationsverket.se for accessibility.
- **However**, Lifos's navigation (Lifos aktuellt, Fokusländer, Sökning, Rättsfallssamling, Länksamling, EUAA COI Portal, Om Lifos) and footer (Kontakta Lifos, Tillgänglighetsredogörelse, Inställningar för kakor, Om Lifos) contain **no link to a "Villkor" or "Upphovsrätt" page**, and critically, **no link to www.migrationsverket.se's "Om webbplatsen" copyright notice either** — unlike the accessibility statement, which *is* explicitly cross-linked. This is a meaningful contrast: Migrationsverket clearly *could* have pointed Lifos visitors to its general copyright notice (the way it points them to the accessibility statement) and chose not to. That doesn't prove the notice doesn't apply, but it means its applicability to Lifos content can't be assumed by simple incorporation-by-reference — nothing on Lifos says "the same copyright terms as migrationsverket.se apply here."
- Lifos runs on the same SiteVision CMS platform family as the main site (evident from shared JS/CSS asset paths, `sitevision-spacing.css`, etc.) but as a **separate SiteVision site** (its own `siteId`, distinct from the main site's), which is a technical detail consistent with it being administratively a sub-property rather than literally the same webpages.

**Conclusion for Q1:** Lifos = Migrationsverket (same legal entity, same operating agency, no separate ToS-issuing entity to negotiate with) — but Lifos does **not** have its own dedicated copyright/terms page, and does **not** visibly inherit or reference the main site's "Om webbplatsen" notice. Treat the main site's text-copyright notice as **not established** to cover Lifos-hosted PDFs; any redistribution basis for Lifos content has to stand on its own (see §3–4 below), not on the migrationsverket.se "Om webbplatsen" finding from the prior research.

## 2. Where are rättsliga ställningstaganden actually published?

**Published as PDF attachments behind document-viewer landing pages on lifos.migrationsverket.se — not as direct PDF links under the www.migrationsverket.se main domain, and not in a plain static document library either.**

Real example, fully verified:

- **RS/001/2024 (version 2.0)**, "Rättsligt ställningstagande. Uppehållstillstånd för besök" ("Legal position. Residence permit for visits")
  - Landing page: https://lifos.migrationsverket.se/dokument?documentSummaryId=48364
  - PDF: https://lifos.migrationsverket.se/dokument?documentAttachmentId=50381 (served with filename `240517300.pdf`), confirmed via download: real 14-page PDF document.
  - Landing-page summary text (verbatim): *"Rättsliga ställningstaganden utgör Migrationsverkets uttalanden om hur en författning bör tolkas och är styrande för myndighetens medarbetare. De beslutas av chefen för rättsavdelningen."* ("Legal positions constitute Migrationsverket's statements on how a statute should be interpreted and are binding for the agency's staff. They are decided by the head of the legal department.")
  - "Referens" (citation) block, provided by Migrationsverket itself on the page: *"Migrationsverket, Rättsligt ställningstagande. Uppehållstillstånd för besök - RS/001/2024 (version 2.0), 2024-05-17"*

Cross-checked second example, also fully downloaded and text-extracted:

Three more landing pages spot-checked for metadata only (no PDF re-download needed — same pattern confirmed):

- RS/021/2020 (v2.0), "Kvalitetskrav på offentliga biträden" — `documentSummaryId=47169`

The Lifos homepage's "Aktuellt från Migrationsverkets rättsavdelning" news feed actively lists new rättsliga ställningstaganden and "rättsliga kommentarer" (a related RK-series covering court-decision commentary) as they're issued, with direct links — confirming the RS-series is a live, currently-maintained publication channel, not an archived/orphaned one. Several 404s turned up for older `documentSummaryId` values found via web search (e.g. 47937, 47470, 46768, 47855, 47878, 47299) — Lifos appears to renumber/reissue document IDs over time (consistent with the version-history mechanism seen inside the PDFs themselves), so a stable long-term anchor for a Vistas pipeline would need to be the RS/xxx/yyyy reference number plus version, not the raw `documentSummaryId`/`documentAttachmentId` query parameters, which are not stable identifiers.

**Conclusion for Q2:** confirmed real documents at real, fetchable URLs, entirely on the lifos.migrationsverket.se subdomain — never on www.migrationsverket.se directly, and never behind a separate document-library product outside Lifos.

## 3. Do these documents carry their own copyright notice, or fall under "Om webbplatsen," or carry a restriction marking?

**Neither.** No copyright notice of any kind was found — not Migrationsverket's own, not one incorporating "Om webbplatsen," and no confidentiality/"internal use only"/classification marking either.

- **Full-text search of both downloaded PDFs (RS/001/2024, 14 pages; RS/059/2021, 11 pages) turned up zero copyright lines, zero license statements, and zero confidentiality markings anywhere** — no cover-page "© Migrationsverket," no footer notice, nothing in the document-history table, nothing in the closing page. The documents are purely substantive: a `Fastställelsebeslut`/`Beslutsdatum`/`Gäller från och med` header block, a revision-history table, boilerplate "Fakta om rättsliga ställningstaganden" text, then the legal analysis itself with footnoted references to förarbeten, MIG case law, and other RS-documents.
- **Landing-page metadata is the closest thing to a formal marking, and it points toward openness, not restriction.** Every checked document (5 total: `documentSummaryId` 48364, 47169, 47120, 50161, 50160, 50138, spanning 2020–2026) carries identical metadata:
  ```
  <META name="limited" content="false"/>
  <meta name="publishingType" content="Publikt"/>
  <meta name="accessControll" content="Publikt"/>
  ```
  This matters because Lifos's own "Om Lifos" page explicitly describes a *different*, restricted category that these documents are demonstrably **not** in: *"Vi strävar efter att informationen i Lifos ska vara öppen för alla. Men en mindre del är åtkomstbegränsad. Det betyder att endast Migrationsverkets personal kan öppna dokumenten. I dessa fall har vi tagit hänsyn till dataskyddsförordningen, upphovsrätt eller sekretess. Internt stödmaterial som saknar betydelse för Migrationsverkets ärendeprövning åtkomstbegränsas som 'handläggningsstöd'. Åtkomstbegränsade dokument syns i databasen med ett hänglås. Rubrik, källa och anledningen till begränsningen framgår."* (https://lifos.migrationsverket.se/om-lifos.html — "We aim for Lifos information to be open to everyone. But a smaller part is access-restricted. That means only Migrationsverket staff can open the documents. In these cases we have taken into account the data protection regulation, copyright, or confidentiality. Internal support material without significance for Migrationsverket's case processing is access-restricted as 'handläggningsstöd' [case-processing support]. Access-restricted documents appear in the database with a padlock icon. Title, source, and the reason for the restriction are shown.") None of the checked rättsliga ställningstaganden show a padlock or restriction reason — they are affirmatively in the open/public bucket, distinct from whatever other Lifos content (likely third-party country reports) does get copyright- or confidentiality-restricted.
- **Does the "Om webbplatsen" text notice extend here?** Not established either way — see §1: Lifos never references it, and it would be an extra interpretive stretch to apply a notice about "text på vår webbplats" (text on our website) to a binary PDF attachment hosted on a sub-site that doesn't link to that notice. Don't rely on it for Lifos content.
- **Positive reuse signal**: every checked document page includes a ready-made `Referens:` (citation) block in a fixed format (`Migrationsverket, [title incl. RS number/version], [date]`) — Migrationsverket is affirmatively telling readers how to cite the document. That's not a redistribution license, but it is inconsistent with an "internal only, don't reference this externally" posture.

**Conclusion for Q3:** no notice exists to lean on (positive or negative) — the compliance question has to be answered by classifying the *legal character* of the document under statute, which is §4 below.

## 4. Are rättsliga ställningstaganden the kind of copyright-excluded "official decision" text (like SFS law text), or Migrationsverket's own authored commentary?

**The primary-source evidence leans toward "copyright-excluded official document," not "ordinary authored commentary" — but this is a legal classification question with no directly-on-point case law found, so it remains an inference, not a confirmed fact.**

Swedish Copyright Act (1960:729), verified verbatim from lagen.nu's consolidated text (https://lagen.nu/1960:729):

- **9 § 1st paragraph**: *"Upphovsrätt gäller inte till: 1. författningar, 2. beslut av myndigheter, 3. yttranden av svenska myndigheter och 4. officiella översättningar av sådant som avses i 1-3."* ("Copyright does not apply to: 1. statutes, 2. decisions of authorities, 3. statements of Swedish authorities, and 4. official translations of items 1–3.") — this is the same provision the project's plan doc (§2.2) already cites as the basis for treating SFS statute text and "myndighetsbeslut" as copyright-free.
- lagen.nu's editorial commentary on 9 § reads "yttrande av myndighet" broadly: *"Rapporten med allt innehåll ses som ett 'yttrande av myndighet' även om där ingår ett verk som, när det förekommer i andra sammanhang, omfattas av upphovsrätt."* ("The report with all its content is regarded as a 'statement of an authority' even if it includes a work that, in other contexts, would be copyrighted"), citing Svea hovrätt 1986-04-18 (DT 17, mål nr 215/83) re: a government-commission (SOU) report absorbing an otherwise-copyrighted scientific text. This shows Swedish courts have read "yttrande av myndighet" to mean an authority's own formally-issued report/statement, not narrowly "a submission in litigation."
- **Structural match**: rättsliga ställningstaganden are issued via a formal `Fastställelsebeslut` (establishing decision — e.g. `RA/003/2024` for RS/001/2024, `RA/093/2021` for RS/059/2021) by the head of the legal department, filed in a dedicated decision-document series, with an explicit `Beslutsdatum` and `Gäller från och med` (effective-date) header, and are self-described (identically, verbatim, in both PDFs checked) as *"Migrationsverkets uttalanden om hur en författning bör tolkas"* ("Migrationsverket's statements/pronouncements on how a statute should be interpreted") that are *"styrande för myndighetens medarbetare"* ("binding/governing for the agency's staff"). That is a formally-decided, dated, institution-wide, staff-binding pronouncement about legal interpretation — the profile matches "beslut av myndigheter" and/or "yttranden av svenska myndigheter," not an individual employee's personal scholarly commentary.
- **Fallback argument even if 9 § doesn't apply**: **26 a § 2nd paragraph** of the same Act (https://lagen.nu/1960:729): *"Var och en får återge handlingar som är upprättade hos svenska myndigheter men inte är sådana som avses i 9 § första stycket."* ("Anyone may reproduce documents that are drawn up at Swedish authorities but are not of the kind referred to in 9 § 1st paragraph.") The 3rd paragraph excludes only: maps, technical templates, computer programs, works created for teaching, works that are the result of scientific research, works of visual art, musical works, poetry, and works an authority sells to the public commercially. **None of these exclusions plausibly describe a legal-position PDF** — it isn't a map/software/teaching material/scientific-research output/art/poetry, and it's freely published on Lifos, not sold. So even under the more conservative classification (an ordinary "handling upprättad hos en myndighet," not itself 9-§-exempt), the statute independently grants "everyone" the right to freely reproduce it.
- Cross-check with another Swedish authority's own reading of the same law: Skatteverket's official legal-guidance page ("Upphovsrättsskydd och ekonomiska rättigheter enligt URL," fetched via Wayback Machine snapshot since the live page rejected non-browser requests — http://web.archive.org/web/20210519200326/https://www4.skatteverket.se/rattsligvagledning/341297.html) restates the same rule in Skatteverket's own words: *"Författningar, beslut av myndigheter, yttranden av svensk myndighet samt officiella översättningar av sådana texter är inte föremål för upphovsrätt (9 § första stycket URL)."* and separately: *"Var och en får återge handlingar som är upprättade hos svenska myndigheter men inte är sådana som avses i 9 § första stycket"* (26 a § 2 st). This page doesn't specifically classify Skatteverket's own structurally-identical "ställningstagande" instrument under §9 vs. §26a either — so it corroborates the general rule but doesn't resolve the specific classification question.

**What's missing**: no Swedish case law was found (in this session) that specifically classifies a "rättsligt ställningstagande" / "ställningstagande" (an instrument several Swedish authorities use, not unique to Migrationsverket) as a §9 "beslut"/"yttrande" versus treating it as an ordinary authored document. The classification argued above is a reasonable reading of the statute and its editorial commentary, not a holding.

**Conclusion for Q4:** strong, well-sourced legal argument that rättsliga ställningstaganden are either fully copyright-exempt (9 §, same bucket as SFS text) or, failing that, freely reproducible by statute regardless of consent (26 a § 2nd paragraph) — clearly a different and more favorable category than "ordinary authored commentary protected like a blog post." Not a confirmed holding, though — flag for the M5 confirmatory legal review rather than treating as settled.

## 5. Signals of restricted/internal treatment vs. encouraged citation/reuse?

**All observed signals point toward active public dissemination, not restriction — with one caveat about Lifos's overall content mix.**

Positive signals (encourages reuse/citation):

- `publishingType: Publikt` / `accessControll: Publikt` / `limited: false` on every checked rättsligt ställningstagande (5/5) — no login wall, no padlock icon, explicitly not in Lifos's own "access-restricted" bucket.
- Lifos's front-page "Aktuellt" news feed actively publicizes new rättsliga ställningstaganden and rättsliga kommentarer within days of publication (e.g. two RS-series documents and two RK-series documents dated 2026-07-13/14, visible on the homepage at fetch time), with direct clickable links — active, current dissemination, not a passive archive.
- Every document page provides a ready-made `Referens:` citation block (see §3) — Migrationsverket is proactively telling readers how to cite the document.
- "Om Lifos" states the database's audience explicitly includes the general public, not just caseworkers: *"Vi samlar in, analyserar, framställer och gör landinformation tillgänglig för aktörer inom migrationsområdet och allmänheten."* ("...we make country information available to actors within the migration field **and the public**.") and the aspirational framing *"Vi strävar efter att informationen i Lifos ska vara öppen för alla"* ("We strive for the information in Lifos to be open to everyone").

Caveat / negative-adjacent signal:

- "Om Lifos" explicitly names **copyright** ("upphovsrätt") as one of the reasons *some* Lifos content gets access-restricted (alongside GDPR and sekretess) — confirming Migrationsverket is aware copyright issues can arise for Lifos content in general. This most plausibly refers to third-party-authored material Lifos hosts (e.g. country-of-origin reports republished from other organizations, listed as content in "Om Lifos": *"Lifos innehåller landrapporter från olika organisationer och myndigheter..."* — "Lifos contains country reports from various organizations and authorities...") rather than Migrationsverket's own rättsliga ställningstaganden, but this session did not exhaustively verify that every non-RS Lifos document type is restricted-for-copyright while every RS document is open — only that the 5 RS documents checked were uniformly open. Don't over-generalize this finding to other Lifos content (e.g. third-party COI reports) without separately checking those.

**Conclusion for Q5:** no signal of restricted/internal treatment for rättsliga ställningstaganden specifically; multiple affirmative signals of intended public reach and citation. This supports (but doesn't independently establish) the §4 legal-classification argument — an authority that believed a document category were ordinarily copyrighted and merely tolerated by informal practice would be less likely to actively broadcast and instruct citation of it in this way.

## Open items / not verified

- **No explicit Migrationsverket/Lifos statement of copyright or reuse terms for rättsliga ställningstaganden was found.** The case made in §4 is a statutory-interpretation argument (9 §/26 a § upphovsrättslagen), not a permission notice like the one found for www.migrationsverket.se guidance prose. Treat it as unconfirmed until reviewed by someone with actual Swedish-copyright-law competence — this is exactly the kind of judgment call the M5 confirmatory review (already recommended in `migrationsverket-villkor.md`) should resolve, and it should be given this specific §9-vs-§26a classification question rather than a generic "is this OK" prompt.
- **No case law specifically classifying "rättsliga/myndighets ställningstaganden" under upphovsrättslagen §9 vs. §26a was located.** The classification argued here rests on the documents' own self-description and structural features (formal `Fastställelsebeslut`, staff-binding, institutional), plus lagen.nu's general editorial commentary on how broadly "yttrande av myndighet" has been read — not on a court decision addressing this exact document type.
- **Applicability of www.migrationsverket.se's "Om webbplatsen" notice to Lifos is unconfirmed, not established, and not disproven.** Lifos never references it, so don't assume it as a fallback basis for Lifos content the way `migrationsverket-villkor.md` established for main-site HTML pages.
- **Only rättsliga ställningstaganden (RS-series) were checked.** Other Lifos content types mentioned on "Om Lifos" — "rättsliga kommentarer" (RK-series, court-decision commentary), "Landinformation" country reports authored by Migrationsanalys, third-party country reports, and the Rättsfallssamling (case-law collection) — were **not** individually verified for their own access-control/copyright metadata in this session, beyond noting they exist and that Lifos as a whole has a documented "some content is restricted" mechanism. If any of these become P0/P1 sourcing targets later (project plan §2.1 lists "Lifos 数据库" as P2/M6), they need their own separate check — don't assume the RS-series finding transfers to them.
- **Document-ID instability**: `documentSummaryId`/`documentAttachmentId` query parameters are not stable long-term anchors (several IDs found via web search 404'd by the time of direct fetch) — any future pipeline ingesting these documents should anchor on the `RS/xxx/yyyy (version n.0)` reference number printed inside the document, not the URL's numeric IDs, matching the project's existing anchor-level discipline for guidance-source citations.
- This is primary-source document and statute-text research, not legal advice, and the researcher is not a Swedish-qualified lawyer. Given this gates an irrevocable CC-BY/CC0 publication decision, the M5 confirmatory review recommended in `migrationsverket-villkor.md` remains warranted before acting on the §4 classification argument.
